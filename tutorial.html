🎮 What is Hegemony?

Hegemony is a Discord-based grand strategy role-playing game that focuses on strategic resource management and warfare, nation/world building, competitive and cooperative community events, and roleplay. We have a consistent and close-knit community that we encourage others to become a part of.

📖 What to Read First

First, we recommend reading the Resources and Cities tutorials at a minimum to begin spawning in, so you may make an informed decision regarding starting tiles. In addition, look at the most recent /main map/ to see what territory is available, and familiarize yourself with the /Player Sheet/.

🌟 Spawning

To spawn, you must submit a request in the /Player Spawning/ channel. It will be formatted as follows:

Name:

Make this something that will pull up when we try to @ you and is unlikely to change.

Country Name:

The name of the RP nation you are playing.

Capital:

The name of one of your starting cities you designate as the capital (can be real or RP name).

Government:

Choose bonuses that best fit your nation and/or playstyle from the list described later in the tutorial.

Religion:

Choose a religion from the current list of available religions from the list described later in the tutorial.

Color:

What color your nation will be on the map, preferably a hex code. Neighboring nations that are too similar in color to each other may be required to change.

Map:

You spawn with 20 tiles and 3 cities. Modify the most current world map /here/, notate which tiles you want with black X's and use white circles or X's to indicate which tiles your cities are in, and clearly mark somehow which is the capital. Note that all tiles chosen must be within the 4 tile range around a city.

📦 Resources

Resources are the lifeblood of Hegemony. Gathering and spending resources is how you perform most mechanical actions within the game.

💰 How to Gain Resources

Resources are gained at the beginning of each week, with you receiving (by default) 2 of a resource for each tile you own of the indicated resource type. Then, any upkeep you have will be deducted from your gains. What remains will be added (or subtracted…) to your national stockpile, tracked on the Player Sheet.

The other primary way to gain resources is through Worldbuilding Submissions. You may also gain resources through Trading, Events, and Warfare.

🎯 Resource Types and Uses

💰 Bullion

Used to pay for trading costs, hire and upkeep mercenaries, as well as place cosmetic labels for your nation on the main map.

🍞 Food

Used for overland expansion, with the cost dependent on the terrain you are expanding to. It is also the upkeep for national brigades.

⛽ Fuel

Used to pay for overseas expansion, city upgrades, some Warfare actions, and paying for naval unit upkeep.

💎 Luxuries

Used to forge mechanically binding treaties, high tier city upgrades, and hiring and upkeeping Generals and Diplomats.

🧱 Material

Used to construct and upgrade cities, as well as build and enhance naval units.

⚔️ Metal

Primarily used to build and enhance national brigades. It can also be used to expand into other player's lands without declaring war, as well as protect trades from thieves.

📝 Worldbuilding Submissions

Each week, you may create 3 (by default) worldbuilding submissions, which can be posted /here/, alongside indicating 1 resource of your choice. Once approved, the resource will be added to your national stockpile.

Worldbuilding submissions can be essays about real or alternate history, RP, graphical designs or drawings, maps, audio pieces such as music, speeches, or hymns, each of which must relate to your nation or territory.

Weekly Deadline: The cutoff for Worldbuilding Submissions every week is Friday night. DO NOT post them on Saturday or Sunday.

⚠️ Resource Debt

Though you generally cannot use resources you do not have stockpiled, you may end up negative on resources at the start of a turn if your upkeep is greater than your resource gain. If this occurs, you will start to accrue penalties based on what resource is and how delinquent you are.

Going Bankrupt in more than a single resource in the same week causes a revolution, giving you a randomized government. This breaks any treaties, truces, etc that were held by the previous government.

🏙️ Cities

Cities are the nodes that connect your nation together and are used as fortresses to defend nearby lands, and can be upgraded to provide additional bonuses. They are required for expansion, trade, spawning military units, and creating cultures. All cities have a 4 tile range around them that you are allowed to expand into.

🏘️ Tier 1 City

Cost: 5 Material

Garrison: 4 Heavy

Naval Range: 2 tiles

Tier 1 Cities cannot build ships

🏙️ Tier 2 City

Cost: 10 Material, 5 Fuel, 5 Luxuries

Garrison: 6 Heavy

Naval Range: 6 Tiles

+1 resource submission per week (up to 3 times)

🌆 Tier 3 City

Cost: 15 Material, 10 Fuel, 10 Luxuries

Garrison: 8 Heavy

Naval Range: 12 tiles

All resource tiles within 4 tiles of this city produce one additional resource of that type. This effect does not stack.

🏛️ Tier 4 City

Cost: 20 Material, 10 Fuel, 20 Luxuries

Garrison: 10 Heavy

Naval Range: 20 tiles

All resource tiles within 4 tiles of this city produce one additional resource of that type. +1 resource submission per week.

Only ONE Tier 4 City may be built per nation in the entire game!

👑 Capital City

One city in your country is designated as the Capital. It has an additional Heavy Brigade as a Garrison and is referenced in many abilities. You choose your capital upon spawn, and may move it for free once every 2 months as long as you are not at war.

🗺️ The Map and Expanding

📋 How to Submit Expansions

To expand, you must have some resources and submit an expansion request /here/. There are three types of expansions:

🌱 Land Expansions

Expanding into empty tiles via adjacent land, cost Food. Must be within 4 tiles of a city.

🌊 Water Expansions

Any that require crossing water, cost Food and Fuel. Limited by Naval Range.

⚔️ Hostile Expansions

Essentially Land expansions into already owned territory, cost Metal.

🗺️ Types of Maps

  • Country Map: The main map showing territory. Updated weekly.
  • War Map: A map with edits making it easier to use for strategizing.
  • Resource Map: Shows the tiles with resources.
  • Terrain Map: Shows the terrain type for each tile.
  • Region Map: Shows regions for the purposes of certain abilities or War Justifications.
  • Island Map: Shows which islands are grouped into single tiles.
  • Canal/Strait Map: Shows which tiles are needed for control over canals and straits.
  • Base Map: A blank map with all white tiles for referencing.

🏛️ Government

Government modifiers are small bonuses that assist with your style of play and help describe your RP to others in simple terms. You have a Government type, which affects how easy it is to change government bonuses and how many you have at a time.

🗳️ Democracy

1 Government modifier

Can swap a single modifier every week for no cost

👥 Oligarchy

2 Government modifiers

👑 Autocracy

3 Government modifiers

Double cost to swap out modifiers

📜 Government Modifiers

Aristocratic: Your first 3 Generals are free and have no upkeep.
Bureaucratic: T2 and T4 cities provide +2 Worldbuilding submission cap, rather than +1.
Confederal: Your vassal limit is increased by 2. Your vassals or overlord gain +2 Bullion per week.
Diplomatic: Your first 2 Diplomats are free and have no upkeep.
Federal: For every region you have a city in, gain 1 Bullion.
Kleptocratic: When in debt, you gain +4 of the negative resource(s) on upkeep.
Mercantile: You do not have to pay the first 1 Bullion fee for trading.
Plutocratic: You may purchase mercenary ships and upgrades, and mercenary generals.
Stratocratic: You may enhance two land brigades for free every week.
Technocracy: You may exchange 3 of the same resource for 1 of any resource.
Tellurocratic: Expansions cost 1 less Food or Metal (minimum of 2).
Theocratic: You have access to Holy War Justification.
Thalassocratic: -1 Fuel cost per tile for Water Expansions. Ships have +1 movement.
Unitary: The tile your capital is on produces +2 resource of its type for every tier it has.

🤝 Diplomacy

The main way to conduct Diplomacy is through Diplomats. Diplomats can be hired for 4 Luxuries, and cost 1 Luxury to upkeep every month. You may move a Diplomat to another player's nation once per week by requesting it in your Private Room.

📜 Treaty Signing

The premier form of Diplomatic Action, treaties are formal agreements between two or more nations.

Examples include: Offensive pacts, Defensive pacts, Military access, Non-aggression pacts, Trade agreements, Border agreements, Demilitarised zones, Vassalization negotiation, White Peace/Conditional Peace

🗺️ Land Exchange

Land exchange is done using normal Trade mechanics, however with one or both sides offering up tiles on the map in exchange. You may trade up to 6 tiles on each side in a single land exchange.

👑 Vassal Contracts

Vassals are when one nation submits itself to another, surrendering control of its foreign policy to its Overlord.

Types: Protectorate, Dominion, March, Tributary

🙏 Religious Conversion

Diplomats may focus their efforts on the religious conversion of a country, allowing the host nation to convert to the religion of the Diplomat's home nation for a fee of 1 Bullion per 3 city weight.

⛓️ Detaining Diplomats

💼 Expulsion: Sends the Diplomat back to the host nations capital.
⛓️ Arrest: Imprisons the Diplomat until freed. Gives the Diplomat's nation a War Justification.
💀 Execution: Kills the Diplomat, removing them from the game. Gives the Diplomat's nation a War Justification.

💰 Trade

Trade between players is performed by both players agreeing to terms, determining a route and fees, and posting a request /here/. Trades are considered processed after 3 days, not including Sunday, representing travel time and giving people opportunities to steal your trade.

🚶 By Land

Land routes are performed through middlemen. You pay an initial 1 Bullion fee, and then for every nation that is not your target that you pass through you must pay an additional 1 Bullion middleman fee to the nation.

You can also travel through empty tiles at a rate of 1 Bullion per 8 land tiles.

🚢 By Water

Water routes must be between two coastal cities, and travel through Sea Tiles. You pay an initial 1 Bullion fee to pay for the first 4 tiles, and you pay an additional 1 Bullion for every 4 tiles thereafter.

You also must pay 1 Bullion when travelling through owned Straits and Canals as a middleman fee to the owner.

🏴‍☠️ Trade Theft

Trading has a significant risk involved from bad actors, which come in two forms; Middleman Theft and Piracy.

Middleman Theft: Middlemen may choose to detain a trade, cancelling it and seizing one player's half of the trade.
Piracy: Occurs when an enemy or black-flagged ship on the same sea tile as one of the trades steals one player's half of the trade.

🛡️ Trade Protection

Land Protection: A Land trade can be protected by spending Metal equal to the Bullion cost of the trade, preventing any trade theft.
Water Protection: A Water trade can be protected by assigning a ship or armada to escort it, which may then fight the pirate off or flee.

🙏 Religion

Everyone has a religion, which they can choose upon spawning. Each has a set of abilities. There are several base religions, which then can "schism" based on player count.

⛪ Schism & Player Count

Every time this happens the new religions will keep the old bonuses and also get a new bonus. This starts with five people, and doubles every subsequent schism.

Regardless of size a player always counts as at least one player for a religion. However, for every 20 tiles they own over the initial 20, they count as an additional player for the religion.

🌿 Animist

Every month, choose a terrain type. That terrain type gives +1 resources from resource tiles.

⛪ Catholic

+1 free diplomat. Converting to this religion is half the cost. Building/Upgrading a city grants +1 resource. T2/3/4 cities give 1 Bullion per week.

🕊️ Miaphysite

+1 free diplomat. Converting to this religion is half the cost. Cannot be forcibly converted. Hostile expansions into your territory cost double.

🌏 Nestorian

+1 free diplomat. Converting to this religion is half the cost. Negate 3 middlemen in each trade you participate in.

☦️ Orthodox

+1 free diplomat. Converting to this religion is half the cost. Building/Upgrading a city grants +1 resource. +1 Government Modifier.

✝️ Protestant

+1 free diplomat. Converting to this religion is half the cost. Building/Upgrading a city grants +1 resource. Worldbuilding submissions give +2 resources.

🕉️ Dharmic

You do not require a diplomat to make treaties with neighbors. Half cost on treaty creation and upkeep.

🕌 Ibadi

You do not pay more than 4 resources for expansions, once all other calculations are complete. Your Generals start 2 levels higher.

☪️ Sunni

You do not pay more than 4 resources for expansions. Your Diplomats have the option to Forcibly Convert other nations. T3/T4 Cities grant +1 Luxury and +1 Food per week.

🔥 Pagan

After winning a battle, you get one free resource for every enemy Brigade that died. "Blank" Tiles cost 2 less Food or Metal to expand into (Minimum 2).

👁️ Primeval Monotheist

+1 government modifier

🐉 Shangdi

For every 20 tiles you own, you receive +1 resources from your capital's tile for each tier your capital is.

🎭 Culture

You can obtain several additional modifiers and bonuses for your nation based on Culture, which may be real or fictitious. The easiest tier to obtain is a Local Culture, which must be pursued alone.

🏘️ Local Culture

Local Cultures are the dominant ruling culture of a single nation, and can only be held by a single player. They have 2 bonuses associated with them.

Must build at least to 8 City Weight within the area (5-15 tiles large).

🌍 Regional Culture

Regional Cultures consist of 5-6 Local Cultures worth of territory to form, and can be jointly formed between multiple players.

Must build at least to 25 City Weight within the territory between everyone who is forming the culture.

🌎 Continental Culture

Continental Cultures consist of 3-4 Regional Cultures worth of territory to form and add an additional bonus.

They require 50 City Weight within the required territory.

👑 Titles

A Title is a bonus that is tied to a single major character in your RP that is conditional to obtain and maintain. A Player may only have 1 Title at a time.

🎯 How to Obtain a Title

To obtain a Title, you must have a T3 city. After this, you can request a Title by providing:

  • • What the title name is (E.x. "Shogun", "Pope", "Holy Roman Emperor", "The Northern Lion", "Pendragon", "Mandate of Heaven", "The Terrible", "Caliph", etc.)
  • • A brief summary of what the title is, the nature of which will determine the type of bonus you receive.
  • • Idea for how you may lose the title. (E.x. Failing to control certain territory, the death of a particular general, going into debt, etc)

🎉 Events

🏆 Tournaments

Every two weeks, we hold two tournaments, rotating between Essay, Debate, Meme, Roleplay, Graphic Design, and Handmade Art. Tournaments are fantastic ways to gain additional resources and a fun way to test your skills.

🎪 Community Events

Held at the whims of the Mods and Admins, with highly varied topics, rules, and rewards, though will almost always be less than tournaments.

👥 Player Events

Players may submit ideas for events and run them themselves with Mod approval. Both hosts and attendees can get rewards.
