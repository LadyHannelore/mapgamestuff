Introduction

Diplomacy is how nations interact ‚Äî whether it be forging friendships, making alliances, starting rivalries, or crafting betrayals.

This is done through the regional threads. If you wish to engage in diplomacy with more than one nation then it will become an event. If you wish to explore the background of a diplomatic mission you may do it in a long-form roleplay post, and it will be graded. Normal short-form diplomacy will not be graded.

Negotiations must be posted, through roleplaying your deals. A simple "we ally" isn't enough. Make it immersive: a treaty signing, a diplomatic envoy, an exchange of gifts, etc.

Therefore there should be no out of character deals. No "meta" alliances made behind the curtain.

Treaties

A treaty is a formal agreement between two or more nations.

Signing a treaty requires a clear list of the signatories, and this list cannot be changed at any time between posting for approval and the treaty's abolition. Each category allows you to have signed a maximum of 3 treaties, with your first in that category costing nothing, your second costing 100 silver, and your third coating 200 silver.

If you want to include more people into your treaty, you must abolish the current one and resubmit it as a new treaty. The above costs will apply if applicable. Everyone who wants to be a signatory must have been involved with its creation in RP or diplomacy.

Treaties with multiple mechanical clauses must be posted as separate treaties in their respective categories. This means that if you are already capped in one that would apply, you cannot sign the treaty.

Breaking a treaty gives the other signatories a diplomatic insult justification.

Unions cannot sign treaties as one unit and must be separate signatories on treaties.

A treaty naturally comes to an end when all of the clauses have been fulfilled.

Border agreements are how nations regulate their expansion

üìú Land Cessions

  • Land cession cover the trading of land between two countries
  • You may not conduct land trades with a nation you are usually unable to trade with
  • Land cessions follow the usual trading rules
  • The land will not be transferred until the next cycle but the resource/silver deduction will be instant
  • if you are ceding land to somebody you do not border but can trade with, they must pay sea travel costs
  • You may only trade 3 tiles per cycle

ü§ù Peace Treaties

  • Peace treaties must follow the justification victory or defeat terms.
  • When calculating peace treaties, cities cost a number of additional tiles equal to their tier.
  • You must follow all standard expansion rules during peace treaty submissions, but the tiles themselves do not need to be congruous
  • You may not take a player's last city unless the peace treaty would result in a full annexation. You are allowed to envelop the city tile.

  • Non-aggression pacts prevent piracy or the declaration of war
  • A party retains their right to hold another signatory accountable if they break the pact in roleplay

üö∂‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è Military Access

  • Military access allows brigades and armies to enter other peoples borders
  • Military access also lets a nation dock their ships in your harbours

üõ°Ô∏è Defensive Pacts

  • Defensive Pacts are bilateral agreements to deter aggression by a third party
  • Defensive Pacts must have a target. You declare a single player as being the person you are signing to defend against, and only if they attack a signatory are you allowed to trigger the pact
  • You cannot trigger a defensive pact unless the war was declared that action cycle.
  • You may not sign a defensive pact with a nation at war
  • The target of the defensive pact may be outlined in a secret clause
  • If you have defensive pacts with two nations at war you can choose which one to activate

üö´ü™ñ Demilitarised Zones

  • Once a tile is declared to be a demilitarised zone, signatories are unable to conduct any such military actions or roleplay in that tile without breaking the treaty
  • Trenches, Watchtowers and Forts may not be built here

üé≠ Roleplay Treaties

  • These treaties only affect roleplay
  • Roleplay treaties don't have a cap or exponential cost
  • If you roleplay breaking these treaties other signatories still get a diplomatic insult justification

Secret Clauses

You may either sign secret treaties or secret clauses within public treaties e.g. Molotov-Ribentrop Pact

If it is the former you must state what type of treaty you are signing and it will count towards that cap

If it is the latter then it is completely free of charge

Only people invited by the treaty writer can see the channel

Secret Clauses by themselves have no cap

How to Make a Treaty

  1. Roleplay the negotiation in üë•-diplomacy. Have a meeting, an exchange of envoys, or a summit.
  2. Write the terms. List clear, understandable clauses from the allowed in treaties list.
  3. Post the treaty in the ü§ù-treaties channel
  4. If you have any secret clauses, submit a ticket
  5. If you desire for the treaty to be mechanically enforced, both parties must agree prior, otherwise breaking the treaty will have no mechanical penalties
  6. Both/all parties must react or post acceptance.

Tributaries

As part of a Peace deal, or by manipulation through diplomacy, you may acquire tributaries. These tributaries:

  • Act as resource or silver slaves
  • Cannot simply refuse to provide you with your tribute, but will always have access to the Independence Justification
  • Are able to garner support from your neighbors, granting them the Liberation Justification
  • Cannot have land annexed
  • Are able to be wholly traded as part of a land trade
  • May be garrisoned by 3 overlord brigades

Acquiring a Tributary is simple, you either win a war where that is a peace treaty option, or they willingly submit to you through a diplomatic treaty. You must be adjacent by land or sea, or have coastal cities within tiles to establish a tributary diplomatically

You can never have more than 2 tributaries at a time, but your tributaries may have tributaries of their own.

Tributaries are mechanically independent from your nation, so their status will not affect expansions, trade, or military action. The overlord may committ 3 brigades to them, and may use those brigades in any wars involving the tributary.

If one tributary declares an independence war, the other may join them against you should they decide to. Independence and Liberation wars are the only way to end a Tributary unless the overlord pays 50 silver in reparation fees to release them. A Tributary declaring a war, being lost, or being released, reverts the brigades to the standard national army of the overlord.

A Tributary must pay either 40 silver, OR 5 resources, to their overlord every map cycle. This is determined by the overlord.

You may trade a tributary to another player, following the standard land trading rules. The tributary retains all land and the treaty is shifted to the new player. Tributaries may also be taken following a war in place of land.

Unions: Shared Banners, Shared Battles

Unions are tightly connected nations acting as one. They provide military coordination, shared purpose, and a unique channel.

Union Formation Rules:

  • Max 3 players per Union.
  • Each member must have at least one Tier 2 City.
  • All members must be land-adjacent, with at least one city in adjacent territory.
  • No overseas or sea-tile adjacency allowed.
  • Each player may be in only one Union at a time.
  • It counts toward your 3-treaty-per-category cap (the first is free, and the second/third costs silver or gems).
  • Requires: A Union Constitution RP post in ü§ù-treaties
  • A Union Charter submitted via Mod Ticket
  • A 50 silver fee per player

Union Perks

Shared Military Support

  • Union members may send up to 3 brigades to assist in each other's wars.
  • No justification needed ‚Äî treated as honorary defenders.
  • Brigades must start in adjacent or allied land and are locked in until the war ends.

Trade Efficiency

  • Land-based trades between Union members are exempt from the middleman tax.
  • City-to-city rules still apply‚Äîno exemptions for sea or naval trades.

Union Constitution & RP Opportunities

Every Union must have a brief written Constitution, including:

  • Shared goals and structure
  • Conflict resolution method (unanimous, majority, etc.)
  • Cultural or RP flavor

Exceptional Constitutions may unlock unique RP-based rewards or quests at the mod's discretion.

Prohibited or Restricted

  • No brigade cap bonuses
  • No shared garrisons or installations
  • No "Union of Unions" or indirect bloc cooperation
  • No internal conflict ‚Äî you must dissolve the Union before declaring war on a member.
  • No extra RP silver rewards ‚Äî Union RP counts toward your 10 bi-weekly cap.

Union Decay

A Union will be automatically dissolved if, for three complete map cycles, its members don't do at least one of the following:

  • Participate in wars (offensive or defensive),
  • Make trades outside the Union,
  • Post Union-related RP or Ranked Submissions.

Dissolution & Leaving

  • A Union can be voluntarily dissolved at any time.
  • A player may leave early by paying 100 silver.
  • Leaving or disbanding triggers a 2-cycle cooldown before joining or forming another Union.

How to Form a Union

RP your Constitution in ü§ù-treaties. Must include:

  • All member names
  • Union Charter terms
  • Shared goals and RP flavor

Submit the treaty with:

  • Confirmation of land adjacency and Tier 2 City status
  • 50 silver per player payment

Once approved, you'll receive:

  • A custom Union Role
  • A private Union Channel for RP and coordination

Unions are for those who don't just want to win ‚Äî they want to shape the world together. Play it right, and your Union might become a legend.

‚öîÔ∏è War Justifications

Every offensive war requires a valid justification. Each has specific requirements, victory/defeat conditions, and peace terms. Important: Losing all of your cities as the Attacker will instantly trigger the defeat conditions.

üè¥ Adjacency

Requirements: Adjacent nation

Victory Conditions: Capture 1/2 cities within 5 land tiles of your territory

Victory Peace Terms: 10 tiles

Defeat Conditions: Fail to capture all required cities within 8 action cycles

Defeat Peace Terms: 4 tiles

Special Conditions: You must be Pagan to use this Justification. You must be adjacent to a nation for a full map cycle before you may use this Justification.

ü§ù Coalition War

Requirements: An adjacent player is 3x your size in tiles

Victory Conditions: Capture every enemy city

Victory Peace Terms: 10 tiles for every coalition player

Defeat Conditions: Fail to capture every city in 20 action cycles, or lose 1/3 coalition city weight

Defeat Peace Terms: Each player loses 3 tiles

Special Conditions: Any player adjacent to either you or your opponent may join your coalition at the start of the war. This coalition may not exceed the tile size of your opponent at the war start.

üåç Colonization

Requirements: A player has land within 20 sea tiles in a region you do not have any land

Victory Conditions: Capture 3 cities in that region, or all cities in that region

Victory Peace Terms: 20 tiles within the declared region

Defeat Conditions: Fail to capture the cities within 16 action cycles

Defeat Peace Terms: Pay 160 silver

Special Conditions: You may only use this if you fully own the region you count the sea tiles from. Union members count as owned land for this. If the defender has no cities in that region you automatically win after 5 action cycles, unless they build one.

üí¢ Diplomatic Insult

Requirements: A player has done something in RP or Diplomacy that you consider offensive to your ruler or people

Victory Conditions: Capture a city

Victory Peace Terms: 2 tiles and 30 silver, and a resolution to the offensive RP or Diplomacy

Defeat Conditions: Lose a city

Defeat Peace Terms: 2 tiles and 30 silver

Special Conditions: None

üëë Establish Tributary

Victory Conditions: Capture 1/2 of their city weight

Victory Peace Terms: Establish them as a Tributary, and take 50 silver or 5 resources

Defeat Conditions: Fail to capture 1/2 City weight within 8 cycles

Defeat Peace Terms: Pay 50 silver and 5 resources

Special Conditions: May not declare war if you already have 2 tributaries

‚ú® Holy Revanchism

Requirements: Your religion at one point in the game has held the territory of the different religion player you are attacking

Victory Conditions: Occupy all cities within the territory declared, and 1/2 of all city weight of all defending players

Victory Peace Terms: All territory declared will be split amongst the attacking Nations as they agree upon it. This must still follow standard expansion rules.

Defeat Conditions: Fail to occupy all objectives within 20 action cycles

Defeat Peace Terms: All attacking Nations must each pay 50 silver to the defending Nations, split evenly amongst them

Special Conditions: This is only available to abrahamics. All same religion Nations of both sides may join the war on the affiliated side. If the nation you are at war with converts to your religion within 4 action cycles of the war's declaration, the war ends immediately in a white peace.

‚ö° Holy War

Requirements: Adjacent or 3 sea tile distance nation with a different religion

Victory Conditions: Occupy 1/2 of all enemy city weight

Victory Peace Terms: They must convert to your religion, and 6 tiles

Defeat Conditions: Fail to occupy 1/2 of all city weight within 12 action cycles

Defeat Peace Terms: You must pay 20 silver per tier for every of their occupied cities, as a minimum of 60 silver

Special Conditions: Other nations following your religion may pay your defeat costs. When a Holy War is declared, any nations following the religion of the attacker or defender may instantly transfer them 10 silver with no trade costs. If the nation you are at war with converts to your religion within 4 action cycles of the war's declaration, the war ends immediately in a white peace.

Defeat Conditions: Lose 1/2 of your coastal cities, and 1/4 of your total city weight

Defeat Peace Terms: 1/4 of all stockpiled resources and a T1 or T2 coastal city within 5 sea tiles, OR 1/4 of all stockpiled resources and a full strait or canal, OR 100 silver and 1/3 of all stockpiled resources, OR 150 silver, OR 1/2 all stockpiled resources
