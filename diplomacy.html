ü§ù Diplomacy

The main way to conduct Diplomacy is through Diplomats. Diplomats can be hired for 4 Luxuries, and cost 1 Luxury to upkeep every month. You may move a Diplomat to another player's nation once per week by requesting it in your Private Room. While in a nation, Diplomats can perform different Diplomatic Missions to accomplish goals. In addition, whenever you send a Diplomat to a nation, a private channel is opened up between the two parties to speak.

There are 4 types of Diplomatic Missions; Treaty Signing, Land Exchange, Vassal Contracts, and Religious Conversion. A Diplomat may only perform 1 Diplomatic Mission a week.

üìú Treaty Signing

The premier form of Diplomatic Action, treaties are formal agreements between two or more nations. Signing a treaty requires a clear list of the signatories, and this list cannot be changed at any time between posting for approval and the treaty's abolition. Treaties always have a cost in Luxuries which varies depending on the complexity, potential effects, and number of signatories.

Breaking a treaty gives the other signatories a War Justification.

Examples of possible treaties include, but are not limited to:

üìù Treaty Creation Process

Treaties are established through a series of stages:

  1. 1. You determine what the treaty is for.
  2. 2. You determine the core specific thing you want out of the treaty.
  3. 3. You determine any additional parts of the treaty you want to include.
  4. 4. You determine trigger conditions for each thing in the treaty.
  5. 5. You determine how long the treaty lasts, and any ways to end it early.
  6. 6. You determine enforced penalties for breaking the treaty.

Once the treaty is approved and all parties sign with üñäÔ∏è, the treaty goes into effect.

ü§ê Secret Treaties

By default, treaties are announced after they go into effect. However, they may also be signed in secret, which costs additional Luxuries. Secret treaties are not announced, however any foreign Diplomats in a secret treaty's signatory nation has a 1-in-6 chance every week of discovering the treaty.

üïäÔ∏è Peace Treaties

When you totally defeat someone in war, you can force a peace treaty upon your opponent without the need for a Diplomat, so long as you don't exceed the victory conditions of your War Justification.

üó∫Ô∏è Land Exchange

Land exchange is done using normal Trade mechanics, however with one or both sides offering up tiles on the map in exchange. You may trade up to 6 tiles on each side in a single land exchange.

Like normal Trades, if a Land exchange is deemed too outrageously one sided or metagaming, it may be denied.

üëë Vassal Contracts

Vassals are when one nation submits itself to another, surrendering control of its foreign policy to its Overlord. Vassal contracts can be forced through Warfare, or negotiated through Diplomats.

By default, you may only have 2 Vassals.

üìã Vassal Rules

  • ‚Ä¢ Becoming a Vassal of a specific type may be a condition of a Treaty.
  • ‚Ä¢ Vassals are not able to have Vassals of their own, and if an Overlord is vassalized, all of their vassals are instantly made independent.
  • ‚Ä¢ All Vassals surrender control of their foreign policy, rendering them unable to sign (non-secret) treaties, declare war, or make Hostile Expansions without permission from their Overlord.
  • ‚Ä¢ The Overlord has military access with all Vassals.
  • ‚Ä¢ All Vassals may not declare war against their Overlord with the exception of the Independence War Justification, or joining with another nation's Liberation CB.

üõ°Ô∏è Protectorate

The Overlord must defend a Protectorate Vassal, automatically joining any offensive wars against them as a joint defender.

üèõÔ∏è Dominion

Dominions may perform non-military related Diplomatic Missions without permission from their Overlord. (Non-military treaties, Religious Conversion, Land Exchanges, etc.)

‚öîÔ∏è March

March Vassals may have an additional 4 units that do not require upkeep (in addition to your 5 base), and receive +1 to all Skirmish rolls when fighting in their Overlord's wars. They must join all of their Overlords wars, offensive or defensive, and Overlords may join defensive March wars.

ÔøΩ Tributary

Tributary Vassals must give 2 of each resource to their Overlord per week. This is processed as a normal trade, though the Overlord is not obligated to give anything back beyond their half of the transit fee. Tributaries may declare war without permission from their Overlord.

üôè Religious Conversion

Diplomats may focus their efforts on the religious conversion of a country, allowing the host nation to convert to the religion of the Diplomat's home nation for a fee of 1 Bullion per 3 city weight (rounded down).

Some religions also have the option of Forced Conversion. Every week a Diplomat with Forced Conversion performs this mission in a nation, a +1 counter is added. When the counter reaches +5, the nation is forcibly converted. Diplomats of other religions may oppose the conversion, each giving a -1 (minimum 0).

üèõÔ∏è Congresses

Treaty discussions with more than 2 potential signatories are considered Congresses, which have a few special rules. Any Diplomat that is in a nation may know of a local congress, though only those invited by the host may attend them. Congresses are naturally secret to any diplomats not invited to attend them, however any Diplomats in the congress city has a 1-in-6 chance every week of discovering key information about what is being discussed. Congress may receive a Treaty discount based on the meta-civility, complexity, and roleplay quality of discussion at a Congress.

‚õìÔ∏è Detaining Diplomats

Diplomats in a nation may be detained by the host nation, allowing for three possible actions on how to deal with them. You may request this in your private channel.

ÔøΩ Expulsion

Sends the Diplomat back to the host nations capital.

‚õìÔ∏è Arrest

Imprisons the Diplomat until freed, forcing them to stay in the host nation but denying them the ability to conduct Diplomatic Missions, however they still may spy on Congresses or discover Secret Treaties. May be released at any time. While arrested, Diplomats have a 1-in-6 chance of escaping every week. Gives the Diplomat's nation a War Justification.

üíÄ Execution

Kills the Diplomat, removing them from the game. Gives the Diplomat's nation a War Justification.

    ‚úåÔ∏è Non-aggression Pacts

    • Non-aggression pacts prevent piracy or the declaration of war
    • A party retains their right to hold another signatory accountable if they break the pact in roleplay

    üö∂‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è Military Access

    • Military access allows brigades and armies to enter other peoples borders
    • Military access also lets a nation dock their ships in your harbours

    üõ°Ô∏è Defensive Pacts

    • Defensive Pacts are bilateral agreements to deter aggression by a third party
    • Defensive Pacts must have a target. You declare a single player as being the person you are signing to defend against, and only if they attack a signatory are you allowed to trigger the pact
    • You cannot trigger a defensive pact unless the war was declared that action cycle.
    • You may not sign a defensive pact with a nation at war
    • The target of the defensive pact may be outlined in a secret clause
    • If you have defensive pacts with two nations at war you can choose which one to activate

    üö´ü™ñ Demilitarised Zones

    • Once a tile is declared to be a demilitarised zone, signatories are unable to conduct any such military actions or roleplay in that tile without breaking the treaty
    • Trenches, Watchtowers and Forts may not be built here

    • Roleplay treaties don't have a cap or exponential cost
    • If you roleplay breaking these treaties other signatories still get a diplomatic insult justification

    How to Make a Treaty

    1. Roleplay the negotiation in üë•-diplomacy. Have a meeting, an exchange of envoys, or a summit.
    2. Write the terms. List clear, understandable clauses from the allowed in treaties list.
    3. Post the treaty in the ü§ù-treaties channel
    4. If you have any secret clauses, submit a ticket
    5. If you desire for the treaty to be mechanically enforced, both parties must agree prior, otherwise breaking the treaty will have no mechanical penalties
    6. Both/all parties must react or post acceptance.

    Tributaries

    As part of a Peace deal, or by manipulation through diplomacy, you may acquire tributaries. These tributaries:

    • Act as resource tributaries
    • Cannot simply refuse to provide you with your tribute, but will always have access to the Independence Justification
    • Are able to garner support from your neighbors, granting them the Liberation Justification
    • Cannot have land annexed
    • Are able to be wholly traded as part of a land trade
    • May be garrisoned by 3 overlord brigades

    Acquiring a Tributary is simple, you either win a war where that is a peace treaty option, or they willingly submit to you through a diplomatic treaty. You must be adjacent by land or sea, or have coastal cities within tiles to establish a tributary diplomatically

    You can never have more than 2 tributaries at a time, but your tributaries may have tributaries of their own.

    Tributaries are mechanically independent from your nation, so their status will not affect expansions, trade, or military action. The overlord may committ 3 brigades to them, and may use those brigades in any wars involving the tributary.

    If one tributary declares an independence war, the other may join them against you should they decide to. Independence and Liberation wars are the only way to end a Tributary unless the overlord pays resources in reparation fees to release them. A Tributary declaring a war, being lost, or being released, reverts the brigades to the standard national army of the overlord.

    A Tributary must pay either Bullion, OR 5 resources, to their overlord every map cycle. This is determined by the overlord.

    You may trade a tributary to another player, following the standard land trading rules. The tributary retains all land and the treaty is shifted to the new player. Tributaries may also be taken following a war in place of land.

    Unions: Shared Banners, Shared Battles

    Unions are tightly connected nations acting as one. They provide military coordination, shared purpose, and a unique channel.

    Union Formation Rules:

    • Max 3 players per Union.
    • Each member must have at least one Tier 2 City.
    • All members must be land-adjacent, with at least one city in adjacent territory.
    • No overseas or sea-tile adjacency allowed.
    • Each player may be in only one Union at a time.
    • It counts toward your 3-treaty-per-category cap (the first is free, and the second/third costs resources).
    • Requires: A Union Constitution RP post in ü§ù-treaties
    • A Union Charter submitted via Mod Ticket
    • A resource fee per player

    Union Perks

    Shared Military Support

    • Union members may send up to 3 brigades to assist in each other's wars.
    • No justification needed ‚Äî treated as honorary defenders.
    • Brigades must start in adjacent or allied land and are locked in until the war ends.

    Trade Efficiency

    • Land-based trades between Union members are exempt from the middleman tax.
    • City-to-city rules still apply‚Äîno exemptions for sea or naval trades.

    Union Constitution & RP Opportunities

    Every Union must have a brief written Constitution, including:

    • Shared goals and structure
    • Conflict resolution method (unanimous, majority, etc.)
    • Cultural or RP flavor

    Exceptional Constitutions may unlock unique RP-based rewards or quests at the mod's discretion.

    Prohibited or Restricted

    • No brigade cap bonuses
    • No shared garrisons or installations
    • No "Union of Unions" or indirect bloc cooperation
    • No internal conflict ‚Äî you must dissolve the Union before declaring war on a member.
    • No extra RP resource rewards ‚Äî Union RP counts toward your 10 bi-weekly cap.

    Union Decay

    A Union will be automatically dissolved if, for three complete map cycles, its members don't do at least one of the following:

    • Participate in wars (offensive or defensive),
    • Make trades outside the Union,
    • Post Union-related RP or Ranked Submissions.

    Dissolution & Leaving

    • A Union can be voluntarily dissolved at any time.
    • A player may leave early by paying resources.
    • Leaving or disbanding triggers a 2-cycle cooldown before joining or forming another Union.

    How to Form a Union

    RP your Constitution in ü§ù-treaties. Must include:

    • All member names
    • Union Charter terms
    • Shared goals and RP flavor

    Submit the treaty with:

    • Confirmation of land adjacency and Tier 2 City status
    • Resource fee per player payment

    Once approved, you'll receive:

    • A custom Union Role
    • A private Union Channel for RP and coordination

    Unions are for those who don't just want to win ‚Äî they want to shape the world together. Play it right, and your Union might become a legend.
