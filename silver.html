üí∞ How to Gain Resources

Resources are gained at the beginning of each week, with you receiving (by default) 2 of a resource for each tile you own of the indicated resource type. Then, any upkeep you have will be deducted from your gains. What remains will be added (or subtracted‚Ä¶) to your national stockpile, tracked on the Player Sheet.

The other primary way to gain resources is through Worldbuilding Submissions. You may also gain resources through Trading, Events, and Warfare.

ÔøΩ New Resource Economy

The economy has been completely reworked around six essential resources. Each resource has specific uses and strategic importance, replacing the old silver-based system with a more complex and engaging economic model.

üîÑ Major Change: Resources are now the entire economy and are used to perform most map actions. Silver has been replaced by this comprehensive resource system.

üìù Worldbuilding Submissions

Each week, you may create 3 (by default) worldbuilding submissions, which can be posted /here/, alongside indicating 1 resource of your choice. Once approved, the resource will be added to your national stockpile.

Worldbuilding submissions can be essays about real or alternate history, RP, graphical designs or drawings, maps, audio pieces such as music, speeches, or hymns, each of which must relate to your nation or territory.

Submissions of very low quality or effort may be denied, and text submissions should be at least 200 words. Exceptional work may be nominated into the Hall of Fame, where they will be posted prominently for everyone to see and receive additional resource rewards.

Weekly Deadline: The cutoff for Worldbuilding Submissions every week is Friday night. DO NOT post them on Saturday or Sunday.

‚ö†Ô∏è Resource Debt

Though you generally cannot use resources you do not have stockpiled, you may end up negative on resources at the start of a turn if your upkeep is greater than your resource gain. If this occurs, you will start to accrue penalties based on what resource is and how delinquent you are. The effects are cumulative, so every previous penalty also occurs upon the beginning of the week.

üí∞ Bullion Debt

Unlike other resources, you may actively spend Bullion to go into debt.

  • -1 to -5: You lose 1 Bullion (interest)
  • -6 to -10: You lose 1 Bullion (interest)
  • -11 to -20: You lose 2 Bullion (interest). Your mercenaries refuse to move this week
  • Bankrupt: Set your Bullion to 0, and lose 4 of all other resources. Lose all map labels. All mercenaries desert. Cancel all pending trades. Disqualified from any events that required a Bullion buy-in

üçû Food Debt

  • -1 to -5: All cities under siege instantly have their siege or starve out counter reduced by 1 or 2 respectively
  • -6 to -10: 1d6 random national brigades desert your military
  • -11 to -20: 1d6 additional random national brigades desert your military
  • Bankrupt: Set your Food to 0, and lose 4 of all other resources. None of your expansions from this last week apply. All of your national brigades desert

‚õΩ Fuel Debt

  • -1 to -5: You cannot move any naval units this week
  • -6 to -10: Any ships not in a port have a 50/50 chance of being destroyed
  • -11 to -20: You cannot move any military units this week
  • Bankrupt: Set your Fuel to 0, and lose 4 of all other resources. All of your ships are destroyed. Lose any overseas territory without a city connection

üíé Luxuries Debt

  • -1 to -5: Your Generals and Diplomats have a 50/50 chance of refusing to work this week. Brigades are unaffected
  • -6 to -10: Treaties with a luxury upkeep have a 50/50 chance of being broken by your nation
  • -11 to -20: Your T3/T4 cities have a 50/50 chance of downgrading to a lower tier
  • Bankrupt: Set your Luxuries to 0, and lose 4 of all other resources. All of your Generals and Diplomats desert. All treaties are broken

üß± Material Debt

  • -1 to -5: Any units that require a Material upkeep do not function this week
  • -6 to -10: Your T1 cities have a 1-in-4 chance of being destroyed, along with appropriate territory
  • -11 to -20: Your T2/T3/T4 cities have a 1-in-4 chance of downgrading to a lower tier
  • Bankrupt: Set your Material to 0, and lose 4 of all other resources. All of your units that require a Material upkeep are destroyed. All of your ships are destroyed

‚öîÔ∏è Metal Debt

  • -1 to -5: All land brigades have -1 Pitch this week
  • -6 to -10: All land brigades have -3 Skirmish and Defense this week
  • -11 to -20: All land brigades have an additional -2 Pitch this week
  • Bankrupt: Set your Metal to 0, and lose 4 of all other resources. All of your land brigades cannot effectively fight this week, and automatically lose all engagements. All sieges you are currently performing are broken. Any brigades with a metal upkeep are destroyed

Going Bankrupt in more than a single resource in the same week causes a revolution, giving you a randomized government. This breaks any treaties, truces, etc that were held by the previous government.

üè≠ Resource Generation

Resources are generated through various means including territory control, city production, trade, and worldbuilding submissions.

üó∫Ô∏è Territory Production

Different terrain types and resource deposits on your territory generate specific resources each cycle.

üèôÔ∏è City Production

Cities generate resources based on their tier and location. Higher tier cities produce more resources.

ü§ù Trade Networks

Establish trade relationships with other players to exchange resources. Bullion is used to pay trading costs.

üìù Worldbuilding
